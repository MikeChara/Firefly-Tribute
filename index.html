The Great Betrayal

Firefly was cancelled due to a per-episode cost of $1million, the highest of its time, and viewing figures that did not support this cost. Many people believed that, as Firefly was created at a time of internet boom, the traditional ways of recording the number of views did not do it justice. Many of the shows fans watched it online, where their interest was simply not recorded.

Firefly film

Despite this cancellation, Joss Whedon found there was considerable interest in further production in the Firefly universe, and managed to get a film deal with Universal in September 2004.

Post-film moments of note

There have been several periods where hope has been raised for more Firefly. There have been cameos by Nathan Fillion in other TV shows, such as wearing the famous 'brown coat' during an episode of Castle. Perhaps the most controversial moment was Nathan saying he would revive the series if he raised a certain amount of money, shortly afterwards a gofundme page was raised and the fanbase rose to the occasion, only for Nathan to backtrack afterwards More reading here
